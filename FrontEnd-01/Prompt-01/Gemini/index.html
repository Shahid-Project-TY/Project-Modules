The seemingly innocent paper cup harbors hidden dangers to your well-being. It's not just paper; it's a **plastic-lined risk**.

Microplastic Ingestion

The inner lining is made of **polyethylene (plastic)**. Heating releases thousands of microplastic particles into your beverage.

Chemical Leaching

Hot liquids can cause **harmful chemicals** and heavy metals from the cup's materials and ink to leach directly into your drink.

BPA and Toxins

While often marketed as 'BPA-free,' other chemicals used in the manufacturing and waterproofing process pose **endocrine disruption risks**.

üå≥ Environmental Impact: A Costly Habit

From forest to landfill, the paper cup's journey is a tale of **unsustainable consumption** and pollution.

Forests Destroyed

Millions of trees are harvested annually to produce single-use paper cups, leading to **massive deforestation**.

Unrecyclable Waste

The plastic lining makes most paper cups **unrecyclable** in standard facilities, sending billions to landfills.

High Production Footprint

The manufacturing process is extremely resource-intensive, consuming vast amounts of **water and energy**.

üí° Causes & Facts: Why It's a Global Problem

Global Demand

Over **500 billion** disposable cups are used globally each year.

Decomposition Time

Due to the plastic, they take over **20 years** to degrade in a landfill.

Water Pollution

Chemical runoff from manufacturing and decomposition contributes to **water pollution**.

The Plastic Layer

The necessary **waterproof plastic layer** is the main barrier to recycling.
